# teag.me Brand Document

## Brand Core

### What teag.me is
teag.me is a QR code and short-link product for people who want to launch quickly, share cleanly, and actually understand what happens after a scan.

### What teag.me is not
- Not a bloated enterprise QR suite
- Not a generic free QR generator with weak analytics
- Not a retro hacker toy

### Brand Promise
Free QR tracking that stays useful before asking for money.

### Positioning
teag.me is the clean, modern QR tracking tool for creators, operators, events, small businesses, and product teams who need a faster path from printed code to measurable results.

### Core Tension
Most QR tools either look cheap or lock basic analytics behind an upsell. teag.me should feel sharp, trustworthy, and generous.

## Strategic Message

### Short version
Tag it. Share it. Track it.

### One-line pitch
Create QR codes, shorten links, and track real-world scans without getting pushed into a paid plan just to see the basics.

### Elevator pitch
teag.me helps you turn physical touchpoints into measurable traffic. Generate branded QR codes, share clean short links, and see scan activity, geography, and device breakdowns in one clean workflow.

### Product pillars
1. Fast to launch
Create a QR code or short link in minutes.
2. Clean to share
Use short paths and branded codes that look intentional in the real world.
3. Useful to track
Get meaningful analytics without getting forced into an expensive plan.

## Voice And Tone

### Voice
Direct, clear, practical, and slightly sharp.

### Tone rules
- Lead with utility, not hype
- Keep copy tight
- Use plainspoken confidence
- Sound helpful, not needy
- Avoid buzzword stacks

### Good examples
- Free QR tracking without the paywall.
- Create it once. Track it everywhere.
- Upgrade when you need scale, not before.

### Bad examples
- Revolutionize your omnichannel engagement journey.
- Enterprise-grade QR intelligence for modern growth leaders.
- Delightful scans for the creator economy.

## Visual Direction

### Overall look
Premium utility.

The interface should feel fast, clear, and well-engineered. White and warm-neutral surfaces create openness. Dark ink text keeps it grounded. Electric blue carries the main action. Warm orange is used sparingly as a signal accent.

### Typography
- Headlines: Sora
- UI and body: Inter
- Utility mono: JetBrains Mono

### Color direction
- Background: warm off-white
- Surface: soft white
- Ink: deep charcoal
- Primary: electric blue
- Accent: warm orange

### Visual rules
- Use real product visuals to sell the product
- Prefer strong contrast and clean spacing over decoration
- Use motion to support hierarchy, not to show off
- Keep the identity crisp at favicon size

## Logo System

### Logo idea
The mark should reference a tag, not a QR code.

The recommended mark is a compact tag silhouette inside a rounded square tile, paired with a small signal dot. That gives the brand a memorable shape while still nodding to tracking and distribution.

### Why this works
- It is simpler and more memorable than QR finder squares
- It avoids the overused "scan code block" look
- It connects naturally to the product story: tag it, track it
- It stays legible at favicon size

### Mark meaning
- Tag shape: create and label a destination
- Signal dot: activity, scans, tracking, motion
- Rounded tile: modern product utility, app-icon friendly

### Logo rules
- Use the mark alone for favicon, app icon, and tight UI spaces
- Pair the mark with the wordmark for marketing surfaces
- Keep the mark simple enough to render in one color
- Do not rebuild the mark from QR squares

## Wordmark Direction

### Preferred wordmark treatment
Lowercase `teag.me`

Why:
- Distinctive and compact
- Feels modern and product-led
- Keeps the domain-first identity intact

### Wordmark feel
Tight spacing, crisp weight, no novelty lettering.

- Use the brand mark confidently

### Do not

- Make the brand feel like an ad network
